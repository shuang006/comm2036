        • Background of Covid-19 in HK

        • Background

          1th wave of the epidemic

          (late January to late February 2020)

          The first case of novel coronavirus pneumonia was confirmed in a man who visited Wuhan and then took the high-speed train from Shenzhen to Hong Kong.

          2nd wave of the epidemic

          (mid-March to late April 2020)

          The sudden worsening of the epidemic in Europe in March led to the return of Hong Kong residents to Hong Kong,bringing the virus into the community and increase in the number of imported cases from mid-March, forming the second wave of the epidemic.

          3rd wave of the epidemic

          (early July to late September 2020)

          The government allowed overseas seafarers and crew to be exempted from quarantine, some infected seafarers entered the community after disembarkation, leading to a gradual increase in the number of local cases in early July 2020 and a third wave of the epidemic.

          4th wave of the epidemic

          (late November 2020 to late May 2021)

          A businesswoman visited the Starlight Dance Club in Wanchai, after which a number of people who had visited the club were diagnosed infected, which was considered the beginning of the fourth wave of the outbreak.

          5th wave of the epidemic

          (late December 2021 to present)

          The fifth wave of the outbreak began in Hong Kong in late December 2021, and the government allowed Cathay Pacific to operate on a "fly out on a passenger flight and return on a cargo flight" basis, which increased the risk of introduction of the mutated virus.

        • Influence and Solution

          Medical system

          Influence

          The rising Covid-19 infection rate has overloaded Hong Kong's medical personnel, with public hospitals so overcrowded that one healthcare provider must often attend to roughly 20 patients during night shifts. Some patients had been forced to wait outside before finding a place to stay due to the increase in cases and the paucity of hospital beds and staff. They were now exposed to rain and cold, which may worsen their situations and result in hypothermia

          Hot Topics: Amid fifth wave, Hong Kongs public health care system overwhelmed, paramedics overloaded
          Solution

          The government has identified new public housing complexes that would provide 3,000 flats to isolate coronavirus patients as well as roughly 10,000 hotel rooms to relieve the burden on the hospital system.

        • 2) Mask production

          Influence

          The extent of the pandemic has been mirrored in the development of the mask industry in Hong Kong. In the early months of the pandemic, things were disorganized, but as time went on, supply chains became more mature, knowledge and expertise became more dispersed, and the level of competition increased. Moreover, the market of it becoming wlid, many types of mask have been launched to meet the demand of consumers. 

        • Education

          Influence
          The COVID-19 pandemic has caused students to miss the time of studying on campus for more than two years, which makes them feel regretful. Moreover, under the epidemic, most of the classrooms have been converted to online classes at home, which has brought about major changes in learning for students, making it difficult for them to get used to it.As a result, students cannot pick up with the progress of the class and feeling stress and struggled. In the long run, it may cause mental disorder. 
          Solution

          The Department of Health provides some solutions and tips for parents to calm their children, for example, spend more time to talk with them, encourage them, do some exercise together etc.

           

          Employment

          Influence
          Hong Kong's economy and people's livelihood has been impacted by the fifth wave of COVID-19. The latest unemployment rate has climbed to 5.4%, and more than 200,000 people have lost their jobs.
          The Census and Statistics Department of the Hong Kong Government announced that from January to March 2022, the local unemployment rate was 5%, but it rose to 5.4% from February to April; during the same period, the number of unemployed persons rose from 188,500 to 206,100, an increase of about 10,000 7600 people.
          The data shows that the underemployment rate in all major industries, including retail, accommodation and catering, transportation, education and arts, entertainment and recreational activities, etc., has also increased.
          Solution
          Hong Kong Government launched Unemployment Support Programme. The Temporary Unemployment Support Programme, which was introduced in February 2022, gives qualified people who have temporarily lost their jobs as a result of the fifth wave of the virus a lump sum payment of HK$10,000 to help them with their financial burdens while they look for new work. Self-employed individuals, as well as full- and part-time employees, may apply.
          Beneficiaries who may still be working but were forced to take time off due to business closures, such as those at beauty parlors, fitness centers, or limited catering enterprises, are now covered by the extended program.

          • Prevention and Control of Disease (Wearing of Mask) Regulation

          Cap. 599I empowers the Secretary for Health, for preventing, protecting against, delaying or otherwise controlling the incidence or transmission of the specified disease, to, by notice published in the Gazette, impose compulsory mask wearing requirement during the specified period. For the purpose of the mandatory mask-wearing requirement, save for circumstances permitted under Cap.599F at Cap.599F premises, a person must wear a mask at all times when the person is boarding or onboard a public transport carrier, is entering or present in an MTR paid area, or is entering or present in a specified public place or a Cap.599F premises. Citizens are allowed not to wear masks if they are present in outdoor public places in country parks and special areas as defined in section 2 of the Country Parks Ordinance (Cap. 208).Implemented from July 15, 2020

        • Mandatory Nucleic Acid Testing

          Depending on the covid-19 situation in Hong Kong, the Government will issue a mandatory testing notice. Persons who have stayed in the restricted areas or specified establishments on the notice list during certain periods of time are required to complete the mandatory nucleic acid testing.

          Restricted areas

          Restriction Details:

          The government will designate restricted areas and issue declarations of testing and restriction. Persons restricted in the areas will be required to remain on their premises and undergo mandatory testing as arranged by the government, and will not be allowed to leave until the relevant test results are generally confirmed.

          Specified establishments

          Restriction Details:

          If there is one or more new confirmed cases in a residential (including commercial and residential) building or its effluent samples test positive, indicating a possible risk of infection, they will be included in the Mandatory Testing Notice. In addition, if there is one or more confirmed cases in a workplace, it will be included in the Mandatory Testing Notice.

          Applicable persons:

          Persons who have been on any premises in the Restricted Area for more than two hours within the past 14 days, even if they were not in the Restricted Area at the time the Declaration became effective, (including but not limited to residents, visitors and workers) are subject to mandatory nucleic acid testing

          Applicable persons:

          Any person (in whatever capacity, including but not limited to full-time, part-time and relief staff, residents, students and visitors) who has been present at the specified locations during the specified period and time period shall be subject to mandatory nucleic acid testing

        • Rapid Antigen Tests

          Demo Video

          An RAT is designed to directly detect SARS-CoV-2 virus proteins (antigens) in respiratory specimens. It is intended for qualitative detection of antigens in specimens from individuals with suspected infections. As such, it should be used in conjunction with the results of clinical interpretation and other laboratory tests
